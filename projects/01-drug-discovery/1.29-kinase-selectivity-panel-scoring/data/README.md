### File format

Whitespace-separated; lines beginning with `#` are comments and are ignored by the
loader (`src/reference_cpu.cpp::load_panel`).

```
N  NFEAT                       # panel size, number of feature channels (must be 8)
LIGAND f0 f1 ... f7            # the query compound's per-channel feature OFFERS
<name> <bias> r0 r1 ... r7     # one line per kinase: name, bias, requirement vector
... (N kinase lines) ...
```

### Field meanings

The 8 feature channels (indices 0–7), shared with `src/selectivity_core.h`:

| idx | channel | meaning | weight |
|----|---------|---------|--------|
| 0 | donor | hydrogen-bond donor | 3 |
| 1 | acceptor | hydrogen-bond acceptor | 3 |
| 2 | hydrophobic | hydrophobic contact | 2 |
| 3 | aromatic | aromatic / π-stack | 2 |
| 4 | ionic+ | positive ionic contact | 4 |
| 5 | ionic− | negative ionic contact | 4 |
| 6 | halogen | halogen bond | 2 |
| 7 | **hinge** | **hinge H-bond motif** (dominates ATP-competitive binding) | **6** |

- **LIGAND offers** `L_f` = how much of channel *f* the compound can form (small
  non-negative integers). Our compound is a typical ATP-competitive scaffold:
  strong hinge binder, aromatic, a couple of H-bonds.
- **Pocket bias** = a pocket-independent affinity offset (integer).
- **Pocket requirements** `R_{i,f}` = how much of channel *f* kinase *i* wants.

Scoring (see `THEORY.md` §2): `raw = bias + Σ_f min(L_f, R_f)·w_f`; predicted
`pK = 4.000 + 0.050·raw`; a kinase is a "hit" iff `pK ≥ 6.000`.

## Full dataset (real selectivity data)

The real analogues of this synthetic panel — fetch instructions are in
`scripts/download_data.ps1` / `.sh` (idempotent; they print links/instructions and
do **not** bypass any registration):

- **KLIFS** — kinase–ligand interaction fingerprints and the 85-residue pocket
  definition. https://klifs.net (web API; the `kissim` Python package builds IFPs).
  *License:* free for academic use; check site terms before redistribution.
- **KINOMEscan / `KdData`** — competition-binding selectivity across hundreds of
  kinases (Karaman et al. 2008; DiscoverX/Eurofins). Often accessed via published
  supplements or commercial panels. *License:* dataset/provider specific — do **not**
  redistribute; cite the source.
- **ChEMBL** — bioactivity database including kinase IC50/Kd. https://www.ebi.ac.uk/chembl/
  *License:* CC BY-SA 3.0 (attribution + share-alike). Large; not committed here.
- **Drug-Target Commons (DTC)** — curated kinase bioactivity.
  https://drugtargetcommons.fimm.fi *License:* CC BY 4.0.

**Respect every license.** Because these sets are large and/or have redistribution
restrictions, the committed sample is **synthetic** and `make_synthetic.py` is the
stand-in (CLAUDE.md §8). None of the real data is committed to this repo.
