### What the sample is

Eight **water-like triangles** (one center atom + two atoms at the canonical O–H
bond length 0.9572 Å and H–O–H angle 104.52°) placed on a coarse 3.1 Å lattice and
jittered by a fixed, seeded rule. This gives a realistic **spread of interatomic
distances** — short intramolecular bonds and longer intermolecular gaps — so the
radial symmetry-function descriptors light up across several shells.

> **This data is SYNTHETIC and has no chemical meaning.** The atoms are **not

## Full dataset(s) — the real thing

Production NNPs are trained on **quantum-chemistry reference energies/forces**. The
catalog lists the standard public sets; `scripts/download_data.{ps1,sh}` print the
URLs and fetch guidance (they never bypass any registration):

- **ANI-1ccx** — CCSD(T)/CBS energies on ~500k conformers of drug-like molecules.
  <https://github.com/isayev/ANI1ccx_dataset> (CC-BY). Format: HDF5 of conformers
  with energies (and forces in ANI-1x).
- **SPICE** — DFT (ωB97M-D3BJ/def2-TZVPPD) energies + forces for drug-like
  molecules, peptides, and ions. <https://github.com/openmm/spice-dataset> (MIT /
  CC0 per release). Format: HDF5.
- **rMD17** — revised MD17: recomputed forces/energies for 10 small molecules,
  the standard force-accuracy benchmark.
  <https://figshare.com/articles/dataset/Revised_MD17_dataset_rMD17_/12672038> (CC-BY).
- **OE62** — ~62k organic molecules with DFT energetics (verify the current URL).

### Provenance & field meanings (real data)

Each record in those sets is: a molecular geometry (element types + 3-D
coordinates, Å), a reference **total energy** (Hartree or eV), and usually
per-atom **forces** (energy/Å). A real NNP minimizes the error between its
predicted energy/forces and these references. Our teaching demo uses only
coordinates and a fixed network, so it reproduces the **pipeline shape**, not real
chemistry.
