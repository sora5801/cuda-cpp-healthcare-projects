This tiny file lets `demo/run_demo` run **offline, with zero downloads** — a hard

cross-section library and make **no clinical claim**.

### Field-by-field meaning (in file order)

The 15 numbers, in order, are the fields of `SimParams` + the run controls
(parsed in `src/reference_cpu.cpp::load_bnct_problem`):

| # | Field | Meaning | Units |
|---|-------|---------|-------|
| 1 | `L` | slab (tissue) thickness | cm |
| 2 | `n_bins` | number of depth bins along the slab | — |
| 3 | `Sig_s_fast` | fast-neutron macroscopic scatter cross section Σ_s | 1/cm |
| 4 | `p_thermalize` | P(a fast scatter thermalizes the neutron) | probability |
| 5 | `Sig_a_B` | ¹⁰B macroscopic **capture** cross section Σ_a (carrier-loaded) | 1/cm |
| 6 | `Sig_a_N` | ¹⁴N macroscopic capture cross section Σ_a | 1/cm |
| 7 | `Sig_a_H` | ¹H macroscopic capture cross section Σ_a | 1/cm |
| 8 | `Sig_s_th` | thermal-neutron macroscopic scatter Σ_s (the random walk) | 1/cm |
| 9 | `Q_boron_keV` | energy deposited per ¹⁰B(n,α)⁷Li reaction | keV (integer) |
| 10 | `Q_nitro_keV` | energy deposited per ¹⁴N(n,p)¹⁴C reaction | keV (integer) |
| 11 | `Q_gamma_keV` | energy of the ¹H(n,γ)²H capture gamma | keV (integer) |
| 12 | `Q_fast_keV` | recoil-proton energy credited per fast scatter | keV (integer) |
| 13 | `n_histories` | number of neutron histories to simulate | — |
| 14 | `seed` | base RNG seed (history `i` uses stream `(seed, i)`) | — |
| 15 | `gray_per_keV` | teaching scale converting keV-quanta → Gy (**not** clinical) | Gy/keV |

Macroscopic Σ = N·σ folds a representative number density into the microscopic
cross section. The thermal microscopic values behind the defaults are the
textbook 2200 m/s ones: σ_a(¹⁰B) ≈ 3837 b, σ_a(¹⁴N) ≈ 1.83 b, σ_a(¹H) ≈ 0.332 b.
Boron's enormous capture cross section is exactly why a few tens of ppm of ¹⁰B
dominate the thermal capture rate — the physical basis of BNCT selectivity.

The reduced-scope teaching model needs **no** external data. If you extend it
toward a validated code, these are the relevant real sources (from the catalog).

  tests: <https://github.com/openmc-dev/openmc/tree/develop/tests>
- **GATE 10** — neutron transport for BNCT: <https://github.com/OpenGATE/opengate>
- **ENDF/B-VIII.0** evaluated neutron cross sections (what real codes read):
  <https://www.nndc.bnl.gov/endf/> *(verify current URL; large, evaluated)*

  *(registration/request may be required; the download script prints
  instructions only and never bypasses it)*

  credentialed; **never** committed here.

`scripts/download_data.ps1` / `.sh` print these pointers and defer to
`make_synthetic.py`; they download nothing gated.

## Honesty / safety

Everything in `sample/` is synthetic and labeled synthetic. The dose numbers are
a teaching artifact on an arbitrary `gray_per_keV` scale; they are **not** a real
patient dose and must not be used for any clinical decision (CLAUDE.md §8).
