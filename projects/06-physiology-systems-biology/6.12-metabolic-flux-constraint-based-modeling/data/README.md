This tiny file lets `demo/run_demo` run **offline, with zero downloads** — a hard
requirement for every project (CLAUDE.md §8). It is **synthetic**: the numbers are
invented to make the biology legible, not measured from any cell.

### File format

Lines starting with `#` are comments, **except** a `#names:` line that supplies
the reaction labels. The remaining whitespace-separated tokens are read
positionally (parsed by `src/reference_cpu.cpp::load_model`):

```
nmet nrxn                       # dimensions
S[0,0] ... S[0,nrxn-1]          # stoichiometry matrix S, one row per metabolite
...                             #   (nmet rows, row-major; +1 produced, -1 consumed)
lb[0] ... lb[nrxn-1]            # lower flux bounds (per reaction)
ub[0] ... ub[nrxn-1]            # upper flux bounds
c[0]  ... c[nrxn-1]             # objective coefficients (biomass reaction = 1)
```

### What the toy network means

Substrate **A** is taken up and flows through a small branched pathway to a
biomass sink. Internal metabolites `A, B, C, D` are mass-balanced (`Sv = 0`);
biomass and waste are sinks. The reactions (with the flux bound that matters):

| Reaction | Meaning | Bound | Role in the knockout screen |
|---|---|---|---|
| `uptake_A`   | → A (nutrient uptake)  | ub = 10 | **essential** (sole carbon source) |
| `A->B_1`     | A → B (main)           | —       | neutral (isozyme `A->B_2iso` covers it) |
| `A->B_2iso`  | A → B (isozyme)        | —       | neutral (redundant with `A->B_1`) |
| `B->C`       | B → C (main trunk)     | —       | **growth-reducing** (only low-cap bypass left) |
| `A->C_byp`   | A → C (bypass)         | ub = 3  | neutral (spare capacity) |
| `C->D`       | C → D                  | —       | **essential** (sole route C→D) |
| `D->biomass` | D → biomass (objective)| —       | **essential** (the objective itself) |
| `A->waste`   | A → waste (overflow)   | —       | neutral |

Fluxes are in arbitrary units (think mmol · gDW⁻¹ · h⁻¹). The wild-type optimum
is biomass flux **10** (capped by uptake). Knocking out `B->C` forces carbon
through the capacity-3 bypass, so growth drops to **3** — a partial (not lethal)
knockout. See `scripts/make_synthetic.py` for the full construction and rationale.

## Full dataset (real genome-scale models)

Real constraint-based models are **public** but distributed as SBML/JSON files far
larger than this teaching sample, and our simple text loader does not parse those
formats. `scripts/download_data.{ps1,sh}` prints where to get them and how a real
pipeline (COBRApy) consumes them:

- **BiGG Models** — curated genome-scale models: <http://bigg.ucsd.edu>
  (e.g. *E. coli* core, 95 reactions: <http://bigg.ucsd.edu/models/e_coli_core>).
- **Recon3D** — human genome-scale metabolic model (~10 600 reactions):
  <https://github.com/SBRG/Recon3D>.
- **Virtual Metabolic Human** — interactive Recon3D portal: <https://vmh.life>.
- **HMDB** (metabolite reference): <https://hmdb.ca>; **Reactome**: <https://reactome.org>.

All are freely available under their own licenses; none are redistributed here.

## Honesty & safety

The committed model is **synthetic and labeled synthetic everywhere**. It is a
teaching illustration of FBA mechanics — it does **not** represent any real
organism and its "essential" reactions are **not** drug-target predictions. No
clinical or biological claims are implied (CLAUDE.md §1, §8).
